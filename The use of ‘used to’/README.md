# 📚 English Spelling & Grammar Rules: The Expressions of "Used To"

This module covers the syntactic formulas, negative and question structures, and distinct contextual variations of the phrase **"Used To"**. This phrase primarily communicates past habits or past states that are no longer true in the present.

---

## 🔤 1. Core Structural Formulas (Past Habits & States)

When using **"used to"** to describe something that happened regularly in the past but does not happen now, the verb form changes depending on whether the sentence is affirmative, negative, or a question.

### 🔹 A. Affirmative (Positive) Sentences
* **Structure:** `Subject + used to + Base Verb (V1)`
  * *Example:* The **girl used to volunteer** at the wildlife rehabilitation center on weekends.
  * *Example:* That stray **cat used to sleep** on our porch every winter.

### 🔹 B. Negative Sentences
When making the expression negative, the auxiliary verb **did not (didn't)** is introduced. Because "did" carries the past tense, the "d" is dropped from "used," turning it into **did not use to**.
* **Structure:** `Subject + didn't + use to + Base Verb (V1)`
  * *Incorrect:* ~~The girl didn't used to like dogs.~~
  * *Example:* The **girl didn't use to like** large dogs, but now she loves them.

### 🔹 C. Question Sentences
Similarly, questions begin with the auxiliary verb **Did**, which forces the main phrase to drop its "d" and remain as **use to**.
* **Structure:** `Did + Subject + use to + Base Verb (V1)?`
  * *Example:* **Did** the **puppy use to bark** this much when it was younger?

---

## 🔧 2. Crucial Distinctions: "Used to" vs. "Be/Get Used to"

A common grammar trap is confusing the past habit expression with structures that describe current familiarity or adaptation.

| Grammar Structure | Structural Formula | Core Definition / Meaning | Project Example Context |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Past Habit / State** | `Subject + used to + V1` | A past action or state that is **no longer true** now. | The **girl used to ride** horses when she lived on the farm. *(She doesn't ride anymore).* |
| **Be Used To** | `Subject + be (am/is/are) + used to + Verb-ing / Noun` | Being **accustomed to** or comfortable with something. | The **kitten is used to sleeping** with the radio turned on. *(It is normal for the kitten).* |
| **Get Used To** | `Subject + get (gets/got) + used to + Verb-ing / Noun` | The **process of becoming** familiar or adjusted to something. | The **girl is getting used to waking** up at 5:00 AM to milk her cows. *(She is adapting right now).* |

---

## 🔄 3. Main Contextual Uses

The core expression "used to + base verb" is structurally selected for two specific real-world scenarios:

### 🔹 A. Discontinued Past Habits and Routines
Actions that were repeated frequently over a period of time in the past, but have completely stopped.
* *Example:* The girl **used to feed** the backyard squirrels every afternoon before she moved away.

### 🔹 B. Discontinued Past States (Stative Situations)
Past realities regarding feelings, ownership, or truths that have since changed.
* *Example:* The puppy **used to be** afraid of water, but now it jumps happily into the lake.
