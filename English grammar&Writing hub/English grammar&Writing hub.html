Unit: Which vs. That & The Subjunctive Mood 📝

1. Which vs. That

Choosing between that and which depends entirely on whether the information is essential or extra.

Essential Clauses (Use "That")

Use that when the details are critical to understanding the sentence. Do not use a comma.

Example: The laptop that has the Wordle game belongs to Celine.

Extra Clauses (Use "Which")

Use which for optional background details. You must separate it with a comma.

Example: My computer, which is a Lenovo, works perfectly for GitHub.

2. Subjunctive Mood: Was vs. Were

This rule shifts depending on whether a situation is completely imaginary or if it actually happened in reality.

Condition Type Rule Example Sentence
Imaginary / Unreal Always use Were If I were you, I would fix the HTML structure.
Real Possibility Use Was If I was late to the session yesterday, I apologize.

Watch Out!

Even for singular subjects like I, he, she, or it, the imaginary subjunctive rule requires were:

Incorrect: If she was free right now, she would help us code.
Correct: If she were free right now, she would help us code.
