This module covers the structural rules, spelling modifications, and context rules for the **Simple Past Tense**. This tense is used to describe actions, states, or events that were completed entirely in the past.

## ðŸ”¤ 1. Base Sentence Formulas

Unlike the present tense, the simple past tense verb form does not change based on whether the subject is singular or plural. The structure remains identical for all subjects.

### ðŸ”¹ A. Affirmative (Positive) Sentences
* **Structure:** `Subject + Past Tense Verb (Verb-ed / Irregular)`
* *Example:* The **girl adopted** a playful kitten yesterday.
* *Example:* **They rescued** a lost puppy last week.

### ðŸ”¹ B. Negative Sentences
When making a sentence negative, you must use the auxiliary verb **did not (didn't)** followed strictly by the **base form** of the main verb. Never use a past tense verb after "didn't".
* **Structure:** `Subject + did not + Base Verb`
* *Incorrect:* ~~The girl didn't fed the cat.~~
* *Example:* The **girl didn't feed** the cat until evening.

### ðŸ”¹ C. Question Sentences
To ask a question about the past, start the sentence with the auxiliary verb **Did**, followed by the subject and the **base form** of the verb.
* **Structure:** `Did + Subject + Base Verb?`
* *Example:* **Did** the **puppy bark** at the stranger?

## ðŸ”§ 2. Regular Verbs: Spelling Shift Rules

For regular English verbs, the past tense is created by adding an **-ed** suffix. However, the exact spelling change depends on the ending pattern of the base verb:

| Verb Ending Pattern | Applied Rule Shift | Project Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Most standard verbs** | Just add **-ed** | *Walk $\rightarrow$ Walked*<br>The **girl walked** her dog through the park. |
| **Ends in a silent -e** | Just add **-d** | *Chase $\rightarrow$ Chased*<br>The **cat chased** a tiny mouse under the sofa. |
| **Ends in Consonant + Y** | Drop the **-y**, add **-ied** | *Cry $\rightarrow$ Cried*<br>The **puppy cried** when it was left alone. |
| **Ends in 1 Vowel + 1 Consonant** (Short vowel sound) | Double the final consonant, add **-ed** | *Stop $\rightarrow$ Stopped*<br>The **girl stopped** to pet the fluffy hamster. |

## ðŸ¦– 3. Irregular Verbs (The Rule Breakers)

Many common English verbs do not follow the standard `-ed` rules. These are irregular verbs and their past forms must be memorized individually.

* **See $\rightarrow$ Saw** $\rightarrow$ The girl **saw** a beautiful hawk flying above the pasture.
* **Go $\rightarrow$ Went** $\rightarrow$ She **went** to the pet store to buy high-quality bird food.
* **Run $\rightarrow$ Ran** $\rightarrow$ The frightened horse **ran** across the open field.
* **Have $\rightarrow$ Had** $\rightarrow$ The girl **had** three kittens sleeping on her lap.
