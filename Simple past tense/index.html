English Grammar Rules

Mastering the Simple Past Tense: Completed Actions & Shifts 🕒

1. Base Sentence Formulas

In the past tense, the core verb format remains completely identical whether your subject is singular or plural. However, you must pay close attention to structural changes in negative lines and questions.

A. Affirmative (Positive)
Subject + Past Verb (-ed / V2)

Example: The girl adopted a playful kitten yesterday.

Example: They rescued a lost puppy last week.

B. Negatives & Questions
did not / Did + Base Verb (V1)

Negative: The girl didn't feed the cat until evening.

Question: Did the puppy bark at the stranger?

⚠️ Grammar Trap Warning: When using the helping verbs did or didn't, the main action verb must drop back down to its standard base form. Never write or say "didn't fed" or "did barked"!

2. Regular Verbs: Spelling Shift Rules

Regular verbs convert to the past tense by using an -ed suffix, but structural endings change based on the base spelling layout:

Verb Ending Pattern Applied Rule Shift Structural Project Example
Most standard verbs Just add -ed Walk → Walked
The girl walked her dog through the park.
Ends in a silent -e Just add -d Chase → Chased
The cat chased a tiny mouse under the sofa.
Ends in Consonant + Y Drop -y, add -ied Cry → Cried
The puppy cried when it was left alone in the room.
1 Short Vowel + 1 Consonant Double consonant + -ed Stop → Stopped
The girl stopped to pet the fluffy hamster.

3. Irregular Verbs (The Rule Breakers)

Irregular verbs bypass the traditional suffix guidelines completely. Their individual formats must be logged and memorized directly:

  • 👁️ See → Saw: The girl saw a beautiful hawk flying high above the horse pasture.
  • 🚶‍♀️ Go → Went: She went to the local pet supply store to stock up on premium bird food.
  • 🐕 Run → Ran: The frightened horse suddenly ran across the open layout of the field.
  • 🐈 Have → Had: The girl had three tiny kittens sleeping peacefully on her lap.
