## 🗂️ Grammar Units

### 🟢 Unit 1: Subject-Verb Agreement
The subject and the verb must agree in number. A singular subject needs a singular verb, and a plural subject needs a plural verb.

> ❌ **Incorrect:** Everyone have completed the quiz.
> 
> ✨ **Correct Layout:** Everyone **has** completed the quiz.

***

### 🔵 Unit 2: Active vs. Passive Voice
Use the active voice when the subject performs the action. It makes your writing stronger, more direct, and easier to read.

*   **Active Voice:** The developer wrote the clean code.
*   **Passive Voice:** The clean code was written by the developer.

# 📘 Gerunds, Infinitives, and Prepositions

Welcome to the advanced grammar module. This section covers how verbs interact with each other and how to use prepositions accurately.

---

## ⏳ 1. Gerunds vs. To-Infinitives

Some verbs are followed by a **Gerund** (an *-ing* word), while others require a **To-Infinitive** (to + verb). Using the wrong one is a very common mistake!

### 🛑 Verbs Followed Only by Gerunds (-ing)
*Common verbs: enjoy, avoid, practice, mind, post, finish.*
*   ✨ **Correct:** I enjoy **playing** Wordle every morning.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** I enjoy to play Wordle every morning.

### 🎯 Verbs Followed Only by Infinitives (to + verb)
*Common verbs: decide, want, hope, need, choose, manage.*
*   ✨ **Correct:** I decided **to create** a web page on GitHub.
*   ❌ **Incorrect:** I decided creating a web page on GitHub.

---

## 🗺️ 2. Mastering Prepositions

Prepositions can be tricky because they often depend entirely on the phrase you are using.

| Preposition | When to Use It | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **In** | For enclosed spaces, months, years, or cities | I live **in** Hong Kong. / The project started **in** 2026. |
| **On** | For surfaces, days of the week, or websites | I clicked the link **on** GitHub. / Meet me **on** Monday. |
| **At** | For specific times or precise locations | The class starts **at** 9:00 AM. / He is **at** school. |

## ⚡ Quick Check: Spot the Error!

Which of these sentences handles both rules perfectly?

*   [ ] **A)** I am planning on avoiding to make mistakes in grammar.
*   [ ] **B)** I am planning on avoiding making mistakes in grammar.

<details>
<summary><b>💡 Click here to see if you're right!</b></summary>

### 🎉 The correct answer is B!
**Explanation:** 
1. The preposition **"on"** must be followed by a gerund (*avoiding*).
2. The verb **"avoid"** must *also* be followed by a gerund (*making*). 
Therefore, *"avoiding making"* is structurally perfect!
</details>
